## Social Norms

- Politeness and queue culture are strong in public settings.
- Service tone is generally friendly but efficient.
- Personal space expectations are higher than in some high-density destinations.

## Language Context

- English dominates in most provinces.
- French is central in Quebec and visible in federal environments nationwide.
- In visitor-heavy zones, bilingual service is common but not guaranteed everywhere.

## Tipping and Payments

- Sit-down service usually expects 15-20% tip range.
- Card-first payment culture is standard in most cities.
- Contactless is widely accepted.

## Practical Culture Notes

- Small talk in service interactions is normal.
- Strong seasonal behavior shifts: city rhythm changes between winter and summer.
- Outdoor culture is part of identity, especially in western provinces.

## Respect and Awareness

- Learn basic context for Indigenous lands and local place names where possible.
- Treat wildlife and protected nature areas with strict respect.
- Follow local regulations in parks and public spaces.
