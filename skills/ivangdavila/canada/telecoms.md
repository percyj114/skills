## Main Options

- **National carriers:** Broad coverage, usually higher prices
- **Discount brands:** Better value in cities, variable rural performance
- **eSIM providers:** Strong option for short trips with data-first needs

## Typical Traveler Approach

1. Compare eSIM first for short stays.
2. If staying longer, evaluate prepaid local plans.
3. Check rural/mountain coverage before committing.

## Practical Notes

- Canada mobile plans are often pricier than many other regions.
- Downtown performance is usually strong; remote coverage is not guaranteed.
- Hotel and cafe Wi-Fi quality varies more than expected in peak periods.

## What to Check Before Purchase

- Data cap and throttling rules
- Hotspot allowance
- International calling needs
- Coverage maps for planned road segments
