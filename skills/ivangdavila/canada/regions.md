## Provinces at a Glance

| Province | Trip Identity |
|----------|---------------|
| Ontario | Big-city access + lakes + mixed cultural routes |
| Quebec | French-first culture, strong food identity, historic cities |
| British Columbia | Ocean and mountain blend, outdoor-first pace |
| Alberta | Rockies access, road-trip infrastructure |
| Nova Scotia | Coastal drives, seafood, maritime towns |
| New Brunswick | Bay landscapes and quieter pace |
| Prince Edward Island | Compact island routes and summer rhythm |
| Manitoba | Prairie context plus Winnipeg urban culture |
| Saskatchewan | Wide-open landscapes and lower crowd pressure |
| Newfoundland and Labrador | Rugged coastlines and remote-feeling routes |

## Territories

| Territory | Travel Identity |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Yukon | Aurora and wilderness routes |
| Northwest Territories | Northern experiences, high-logistics planning |
| Nunavut | Remote and specialized expedition-style travel |

## Regional Planning Rule

Pick 1-2 major regions per trip unless you have significant time. Canada's scale punishes over-ambitious routing.
