# Dubai Local & Regional Cuisine

## Emirati Cuisine

**Reality check:** Authentic Emirati restaurants are RARE. Emirati food is traditionally home-cooked, and the cuisine is relatively simple compared to neighboring countries. Most tourists never try real Emirati food.

### Traditional Emirati Dishes

| Dish | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Machboos** | Spiced rice with meat/chicken (like biryani but distinct spice profile) |
| **Harees** | Wheat porridge with meat, slow-cooked until smooth |
| **Thareed** | Meat stew with bread and vegetables (Emirati comfort food) |
| **Jesheed** | Dried shark cooked with spices |
| **Luqaimat** | Sweet dumplings with date syrup (dessert) |
| **Balaleet** | Sweet vermicelli with eggs (breakfast) |
| **Karak chai** | Strong cardamom tea with condensed milk |

## Where Locals Actually Eat

**Budget spots locals love:**
- **Ravi Restaurant** (Satwa) — Pakistani, open 24h, always packed
- **Al Mallah** (Satwa) — Shawarma, juices
- **Karama cafeterias** — Any small Pakistani/Indian restaurant
- **3Fils** — Japanese small plates at Jumeirah Fishing Harbour (need to book)
- **Blu Pizzeria** — Umm Suqeim fish harbor, 6 pizzas only, walk-in

**Note:** Most Emiratis dine at home or at private gatherings. They frequent luxury hotel restaurants for special occasions.
