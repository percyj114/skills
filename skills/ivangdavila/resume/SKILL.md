## The 6-Second Test

Recruiters scan resumes in 6 seconds. Before anything else, verify:

1. **Title clarity** â€” Current/target role obvious at top?
2. **Impact visible** â€” Top 3 achievements jump out?

## Responsibilities â†’ Achievements

The #1 resume killer: listing tasks instead of results.

**Transform pattern:**
- "Responsible for..." â†’ "Achieved X resulting in Y"
- "Managed team of..." â†’ "Built team from X to Y, delivering Z"
- "Worked on..." â†’ "Led/contributed to X, increasing Y by Z%"

## Tailoring Workflow

When adapting to a specific job:

1. **Extract requirements** â€” Parse job description for must-haves vs nice-to-haves
2. **Map experience** â€” Which of user's achievements match each requirement?
3. **Identify gaps** â€” What's missing? Can it be reframed or is it a real gap?
4. **Inject keywords** â€” Add exact terms from JD, naturally integrated
5. **Reorder sections** â€” Most relevant experience first

See `tailoring.md` for job description parsing patterns.

## Career Changers

When user is switching industries/roles:

1. **Vocabulary translation** â€” Rewrite achievements in target industry language
2. **Transferable skills** â€” Extract hidden competencies ("budget management" = "P&L ownership")
3. **Narrative bridge** â€” 2-3 sentences connecting past to future coherently
4. **Section reorder** â€” Skills/summary may go before experience
5. **Red flag check** â€” Avoid phrases that signal confusion about direction

See `tailoring.md` section on industry translation.

## Senior/Executive Resumes

15+ years of experience requires different approach:

1. **Career arc** â€” One coherent progression, not 12 disconnected roles
2. **Recency weighting** â€” Last 5-7 years detailed, earlier roles condensed
3. **Strategic omissions** â€” Remove outdated tech, irrelevant roles, age signals
4. **Leadership evidence** â€” Show HOW you led, not just that you did
5. **Role calibration** â€” Adjust tone if targeting lower level (avoid "overqualified" rejection)

See `seniority.md` for condensing techniques.

## Red Flags to Fix

Immediate disqualification triggers:

- **Unexplained gaps** â€” Address or the recruiter imagines worst
- **Job hopping without context** â€” Brief roles need positioning
- **Typos in tech names** â€” ReactJS vs React.js matters
- **Generic objective** â€” "Seeking challenging opportunity" = delete
- **Skills mismatch** â€” Claims that experience doesn't support

## Format Rules

- **Length:** <10 years = 1 page, 10-20 = 2 pages, exec = 2-3
- **File:** Have PDF, DOCX, and plain text versions ready
- **Design:** One column safer for ATS, minimal color, no graphics
- **Dates:** Consistent format throughout (MM/YYYY or Month YYYY)

For ATS-specific formatting, see `ats.md`.
