# Finnish and Nordic Cuisine

## Core Finnish Foods

### Fish

**Salmon (Lohi)**
Most important fish. Prepared many ways:
- **Graavilohi**: Cured with salt, sugar, dill
- **Savulohi**: Cold or hot smoked
- **Lohikeitto**: Creamy salmon soup (essential)
- **Grilled/baked**: Simple preparations

**Baltic Herring (Silakka)**
Small fish, traditional staple.
- Fried, pickled, or smoked
- Silakkapihvit: Herring patties
- Silakkalautaset: Herring plates at markets

**Vendace (Muikku)**
Small lake fish, fried whole.
- Crispy, eaten bones and all
- Lake district specialty
- Available at markets

**Whitefish (Siika)**
Delicate freshwater fish.
- Smoked or grilled
- Restaurant staple

### Meat

**Reindeer (Poro)**
Lapland specialty, available everywhere.
- Poronkaristys: Sauteed reindeer with mashed potatoes
- Dried reindeer: Snack meat
- Lean, gamey, sustainable

**Elk/Moose (Hirvi)**
Wild game, autumn specialty.
- Steaks, stews
- Richer than beef
- Seasonal availability

**Meatballs (Lihapullat)**
Finnish comfort food.
- Different from Swedish version
- With mashed potatoes, lingonberries

**Sausage (Makkara)**
BBQ staple, many varieties.
- Grillimakkara: Large BBQ sausage
- Essential camping/cottage food

### Bread and Pastries

**Ruisleipae (Rye bread)**
Dense, dark, essential.
- Everyday staple
- Many varieties
- Best fresh from bakery

**Pulla (Cardamom bread)**
Sweet braided bread.
- Coffee companion
- Often with pearl sugar

**Korvapuusti (Cinnamon roll)**
"Slapped ears" - Finnish cinnamon bun.
- Every cafe has them
- Cardamom-spiced
- Not too sweet

**Karjalanpiirakka (Karelian pie)**
Rice-filled rye pastry.
- Eaten with egg butter (munavoi)
- Breakfast, snack, any time
- Regional specialty, now national

**Lihapiirakka**
Meat pie, fast food staple.
- From kiosks
- Often with mustard

### Soups and Stews

**Lohikeitto (Salmon soup)**
Creamy, dill-flavored.
- Must try
- Every restaurant has version
- Often with bread

**Hernekeitto (Pea soup)**
Traditional Thursday food.
- Military/school tradition
- With pancakes (pannukakku)

**Kalakeitto (Fish soup)**
Clear fish soup variant.
- Often perch or pike
- Lighter than salmon soup

### Sides and Accompaniments

**New potatoes (Uudet perunat)**
Summer specialty.
- With butter and dill
- Seasonal obsession (June-July)

**Lingonberries (Puolukka)**
Wild berries, tart.
- With meat dishes
- Jam form common

**Pickles**
Accompany many meals.
- Cucumber, beets common

### Desserts

**Berry desserts**
Seasonal, wild berries.
- Blueberries, lingonberries, cloudberries
- Pies, soups, with cream

**Cloudberry (Lakka)**
Arctic berry, expensive.
- With ice cream
- Liqueur form (Lapponia)
- Luxury ingredient

**Kiisseli**
Berry pudding/soup.
- Served warm or cold
- Traditional dessert

## New Nordic Cuisine

Modern Finnish dining draws on Nordic movement:

**Key principles:**
- Local, seasonal ingredients
- Foraged elements (mushrooms, herbs, berries)
- Minimal processing
- Nature-inspired presentation
- Sustainable sourcing

**Common techniques:**
- Fermentation
- Smoking
- Pickling
- Fresh herbs

**Restaurant examples:**
- Olo (Michelin star)
- Groen
- Inari
- Palace

## Where to Try Finnish Food

### Best Introduction
- **Old Market Hall**: Samples, small portions
- **Savotta**: Tourist-friendly but authentic
- **Sea Horse**: Classic Finnish, local crowd

### Local Favorites
- **Kosmos**: Modern Finnish
- **Juuri**: Finnish tapas (sapas)
- **Shelter**: Seasonal menu

### Market Experiences
- Hakaniemi Market Hall (local)
- Market Square stalls (summer)
- Hietalahti Market Hall

### Budget Finnish
- Lounas restaurants
- Fafa's karjalanpiirakka
- Supermarket prepared foods

## What to Skip

- **Tourist trap versions** of Finnish food at overpriced harbor restaurants
- **Reindeer everything** - once is enough
- **Finnish Chinese food** - generally poor
